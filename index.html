Noam Levi


I am an AI4Science Postdoctoral Fellow at EPFL. My research focuses on the intersection of theoretical physics and machine learning, borrowing tools from Random Matrix Theory, Field Theory, and Statistical Physics to study ML algorithms. I aim to bridge Statistical Learning Theory, Optimization, and Dynamics with Representation Learning, and I’m also interested in applying ML to advance physical sciences.


Previously:

  • PhD Student - Theoretical Particle Physics at Tel Aviv University, under Prof. Tomer Volansky.
  • Visiting researcher at IAS, Princeton.
